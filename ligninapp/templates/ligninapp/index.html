Pitch (Informal)

Literature reviews are great, but they show the literature at one point in time. It doesn't need to be static anymore, because we have alternative publishing methods now. So, why don't we pool all the informal, ad-hoc spreadsheets we have from our old literature review sections - and plug your own papers on these tables, too!

Pitch (Formal)

Research builds upon the already-known to reach the not-yet-known. Delineating between the two often takes the form of an ad-hoc taxonomy of related work created for the specific paper submission, with some criteria drawn based upon previous literature and some criteria drawn using new distinctions that the authors contribute to the literature. Defining this space implied by several works is often the contribution of literature review papers and SoKs. However, these reviews are static, limiting their usefulness in fast-moving areas. I propose that a dynamic, updating literature review that supports the development of ad-hoc taxonomies can help individual researchers find and sort related work quickly as well as provide shared ground for other researchers to assert or correct these same ideas.
